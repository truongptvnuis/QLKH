1. Introduction
The Warehouse Management System (WMS) is a sophisticated software platform that is engineered to optimize warehouse functionality and distribution center management. It is designed to facilitate and manage the execution of a warehouse's operations, including inventory management, picking processes and auditing.

2. Features
2.1 Inventory Control
The WMS empowers warehouse managers to maintain tight control over inventory levels, track stock location, and streamline picking processes. This aids in reducing the risk of overselling items and ensures the right products are always in stock.

2.2 Order Fulfillment
The system offers efficient order fulfillment features, allowing for fast and accurate order processing. It efficiently manages the picking, packing, and shipping of orders, consequently enhancing customer satisfaction.

2.3 Automation
The WMS is equipped with automation capabilities that enable it to perform tasks such as receiving stock, put-away, picking items for dispatch, and maintaining accurate stock levels with minimal human intervention.

2.4 Reporting and Analytics
The system offers powerful reporting and analytics tools. These provide warehouse managers with insights into the operation's effectiveness and productivity, informing strategic decisions to improve warehouse performance.

User Guide: Warehouse Management System (WMS)

Introduction
The Warehouse Management System (WMS) is a user-friendly application designed to help small and medium-sized businesses efficiently manage their warehouse operations. This guide will walk you through the various features and functionalities of the WMS application.

Login

Launch the WMS application on your Windows PC.
Enter your username and password to log in.
Click the "Login" button to access the system.
Dashboard
Upon successful login, you will be directed to the WMS dashboard.
The dashboard provides an overview of important information such as inventory status, pending orders, and revenue statistics.
Manage Receiving Vouchers
To record incoming stock, click on the "Receiving Vouchers" tab.
Click the "Add New Voucher" button to create a new receiving voucher.
Fill in the necessary details, such as supplier information, item details, and quantity.
Click the "Save" button to save the receiving voucher.
Manage Delivery Vouchers
To manage outgoing stock, navigate to the "Delivery Vouchers" tab.
Click the "Add New Voucher" button to create a new delivery voucher.
Enter the customer information, item details, and quantity to be delivered.
Click the "Save" button to save the delivery voucher.
Manage Inventory
The "Inventory" tab allows you to monitor and track your inventory.
You can view the current stock levels of each item, including quantity on hand and quantity reserved.
Use the search and filter options to locate specific items quickly.
Manage Goods
To add, update, or remove goods from the system, go to the "Manage Goods" tab.
Click the "Add New Good" button to add a new item to the inventory.
Fill in the necessary details, such as name, description, category, and price.
Click the "Save" button to save the new good.
Manage Goods Categories
The "Goods Categories" tab allows you to categorize goods for better organization.
Click the "Add New Category" button to create a new category.
Enter the category name and any additional details.
Click the "Save" button to save the category.
Manage Customers
To manage customer information, navigate to the "Customers" tab.
Click the "Add New Customer" button to add a new customer.
Enter the customer's details, such as name, contact information, and address.
Click the "Save" button to save the customer information.
Manage Suppliers
The "Suppliers" tab allows you to record and manage supplier information.
Click the "Add New Supplier" button to add a new supplier.
Enter the supplier's details, such as name, contact information, and address.
Click the "Save" button to save the supplier information.
Manage Revenue
To track revenue from sales and generate reports, go to the "Revenue" tab.
You can view revenue statistics based on different time periods and generate detailed reports.
Use the filter options to specify the desired time range and view revenue by customer, goods, or category.
Logout
To log out of the WMS application, click on the "Logout" button.
This will end your current session and return you to the login screen.
Note: This user guide provides a general overview of the key functionalities of the Warehouse Management System (WMS). For more detailed instructions and assistance, refer to the system's help documentation or contact your system administrator.

That concludes the user guide for the Warehouse Management System (WMS).
